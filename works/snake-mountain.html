Snake Mountain

Installation. Material: nail tips, tree, stone, acrylic sheet, acrylic paint, watercolour, tung oil

Size: variable dimensions

Time: 2023

Description: I wrapped a tree in nail tips and placed it among the piles of stones, and the “staff” was transformed into a “snake” that shuttled it. Together with the acrylic slices representing water, they form a landscape imagery. I painted some of the aquatic creatures on acrylic, reproducing and recreating their forms as if in a prism. As an inorganic substance, the stone is almost as long as the life of the earth, and it represents a primitive substance in the universe, symbolizing the most primitive memories of the earth. Nails symbolize the transience of existence. After the touch of passing years, nails grow endlessly, and stones are precipitated by the sun and the moon. In the memories of time, only that which is ephemeral is lasting. The multiple dialogues created between nail tips, tree, stone, and acrylic invite the audience to contemplate the profound interplay of time, nature, and human experience.
